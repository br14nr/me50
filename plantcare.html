Symptoms and Remedies

Symptoms Disease Treatment
Flowers of fruit trees wilt and turn brown Brown Rot Spray with Copper Oxycholoride 3 weekly until harvest
Dark patches on top of the leaves, often with corresponding furry or downy growth on the undersides. Downy Mildew Growing plants in open positions with good air circulation, spray with Fungicides e.g. Yates Natures Way
Circular black spots on the leaves of Roses which are fringed with yellow margins Black Spot All diseased leaves should be raked up and disposed of. Diseased stems need to be pruned back to healthy wood. Spray roses with a Lime Sulphur spray in winter and start an early preventative spray program at the beginning of spring once foliage emerges.
